1.2 Definition

  • Let $X$ be a discrete random variable with alphabet $\mathcal{X}$ and probability mass function $p(x) = \Pr(X = x), x \in \mathcal{X}$
  • For convenience, denote p.m.f. by $p(x)$ rather than $P_{X}(x)$, thus $p(x)$ and $p(y)$ are two different p.m.f.s

Definition (Entropy)

  • $0\log 0 \rightarrow 0$
  • $H(x)$ only depends on $p(x)$. We also write $H(p)$ for $H(X)$
  • $H(X) \geqslant 0$
  • When $X$ is uniform over $\mathcal{X}$, then $H(X) = \log |\mathcal{X}|$
  • $H{b}(X) = \log{b} aH_{a}(X)$
    • in which $a$ and $b$ denotes the base of the logarithm in the definition
    • If $b = e$, the entropy is measured by nats
    • Otherwise, it is measured by bits
